Interest in IT

My interest in IT started when my parents bought me my first laptop (HP Envy M6) and played the popular game Minecraft. I became intrigued that an indie game developer (Notch) created a billion-dollar game just from his computer and changed the lives of many people from it being their fulltime jobs or meeting a life-long friend through the internet. As I played the game more and more, I started watching popular YouTubers and saw them playing around with Redstone. It was game-changing for me! It helped grow my creativity, especially in terms of problem-solving and working with what you’ve got. From this moment forward, I discovered that multiplayer existed and built my own Minecraft server. I was incredibly proud of this moment, and all my friends praised me for it. As I grew older, I started to experiment with Java & Eclipse, building simple mods but quickly lost interest. I found it more fascinating, creating and designing websites that could be easily accessed by anyone – and because I got more praise for it. By 2015, my laptop started to deteriorate and began to look into desktop computers. I discovered through my friends that it is possible to build PC’s, and he showed me his epic computer build. Now in 2020, I have made several computers for my friends and family sourcing the parts on either Gumtree or eBay. I believe many teens like me have entered the world of programming through Notch’s inspiring story.

I chose to come to RMIT as it was the only option for me to get a degree and keep my full-time job. I saw that OUA was enrolling for the Bachelor of IT, and it was just lucky that IT and programming are one of my passions I wanted to pursue in the future.

I’m looking to learn, expand my knowledge and skills to add to my portfolio. I mainly want to learn user-experience and app development.
